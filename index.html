Tony Blair is a former UK Prime Minister who served between 1997-2007. He led the party towards the political centre and was responsible for such policies as the Minimum Wage, Devolution and the Good Friday Agreement. He intervened in Kosovo and Sierra Leone, becoming a hero

Ellen Wilkinson was a Labour MP who served as Minister for Education between 1945-1947, under Clement Attlee. She helped introduce the 1944 Education Act and was known for her radical socialism and feminism

Harold Wilson was a former UK Prime Minister between 1964-1970 and between 1975-1976. During his tenure the Labour government banned capital punishment and partially decriminalised homosexuality as well as creating the Open University

Clement Attlee was a former UK Prime Minister between 1945-51, the first elected Labour Party Prime Minister. During his tenure, major reforms were undertaken including the foundation of the NHS, the UK joining NATO, India being granted independence and the major nationalisation of industries such as coal, banking and steel manufacturing.

Ken Livingstone is a former Mayor of London who served in the post between 2000-2008. He was previously the leader of the Greater London Council between 1981-1986, before its abolition. He was an MP between 1987-2001. In his time as London Mayor, he introduced bendy buses, he introduced the Oyster card and introduced the congestion charge, a first for green politics.

Gordon Brown is a former UK Prime Minister who served between 2007-2010. During his time as chancellor he oversaw the period of the longest uninterrupted growth in the history of the UK. He was responsible for funding major infrastructure projects across the country, extending the powers of the Treasury and giving the Bank of England monetary independence.
